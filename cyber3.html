What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyber attacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

Core Pillars of Security

  • Confidentiality: Preventing unauthorized access to information.
  • Integrity: Ensuring information is accurate and has not been tampered with.
  • Availability: Guaranteeing authorized users can access information when needed.
---

Common Cyber Threats

Threat Description Mitigation
**Malware** Malicious software (viruses, ransomware, spyware) designed to damage or disable systems. Antivirus software, system patching, network monitoring.
**Phishing** Deceptive emails or messages trying to trick users into revealing sensitive data. User training, email filtering, multi-factor authentication.
**DDoS** Distributed Denial of Service; overwhelming a server with traffic to shut it down. Traffic filtering, load balancing, cloud-based protection services.
**Zero-Day Exploit** Exploiting a software vulnerability before the vendor can release a patch. Intrusion prevention systems, behavioral analysis tools.

Careers in Cyber Protection

The cybersecurity field is one of the fastest-growing job markets globally, with high demand for skilled professionals to protect against ever-evolving threats.

Key Roles:

  • Security Analyst: Monitors systems, detects threats, and implements defense measures.
  • Ethical Hacker (Pen Tester): Legally tests systems to find vulnerabilities before attackers do.
  • Forensics Expert: Investigates cyber crimes to gather evidence after an attack.
  • Chief Information Security Officer (CISO): Oversees the entire organization's security strategy.
