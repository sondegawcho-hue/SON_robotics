Frequently Asked Questions

What is SON ROBOTICS? +

SON ROBOTICS is an initiative focused on empowering youth for the future by providing education and opportunities in robotics, programming, and cyber security. Our goal is to equip students with the critical skills needed for innovation and problem-solving.

Why is robotics important to learn? +

Robotics is essential for innovation, education, and solving global challenges. It teaches critical thinking, creativity, and practical technical skills. Learning robotics prepares students for future careers and helps them understand the technology transforming our world, from manufacturing and healthcare to environmental sustainability.

What career opportunities are there in robotics? +

The field of robotics offers many exciting career paths, including:

  • Robotics Engineer: Designing and building robots.
  • AI Developer: Creating the "brains" for robots to learn and make decisions.
  • Automation Specialist: Implementing robotic systems in industries.
  • Medical Robotics Technician: Supporting robotic-assisted surgery and healthcare.
  • Drone Operator: Using aerial robotics for mapping, delivery, and more.

The field is growing rapidly, with millions of jobs expected to be created in the coming years.

How can I start learning robotics? +

Learning robotics is a step-by-step process:

  1. Foundation: Start with basic electronics and coding (like Arduino or Python).
  2. Online Learning: Use resources like YouTube, Coursera, and Udemy.
  3. Join Communities: Participate in robotics clubs, competitions (like WRO or FIRST), or local maker spaces.
  4. Build Projects: Get hands-on experience by building simple robots.
  5. Advanced Skills: Move on to topics like AI, machine learning, and computer vision.
What programming languages are most important for robotics? +

Several languages are used, depending on the task:

  • Python & C++: These are the most common for robot control, AI, and using the Robot Operating System (ROS).
  • Arduino (C++ variant): Ideal for beginners and controlling microcontrollers.
  • MATLAB/Simulink: Used for simulation and modeling.
  • HTML/CSS/JavaScript: Used for creating web-based control dashboards.
How do robots help solve real-world problems? +

Robots provide solutions in many critical areas:

  • Disaster Response: Search-and-rescue robots can enter dangerous areas to find survivors.
  • Healthcare: Surgical robots perform operations with high precision, and assistive robots help patients with mobility.
  • Agriculture: Robots automate harvesting and monitoring to increase food production.
  • Environment: Robots can clean oceans, monitor pollution, and track wildlife.
